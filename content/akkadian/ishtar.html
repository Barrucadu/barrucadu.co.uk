The Descent of Ištar (wikipedia) is the story of how Ištar—the goddess of love, sex, and war—decided to visit the Netherworld, got trapped, and had to be rescued by quick-thinking Ea who supplied a handsome man to distract Ereškigal—the Queen of the Netherworld. While Ištar was trapped, living creatures stopped producing offspring, so there's maybe a seasonal connotation there too (à la the Greek myth of Persephone).

My Assyriological hot take is that this is essentially a worse version of the Sumerian Angalta, but I don't know Sumerian yet so I can't translate that. But one day I will!

See also my blog post.
